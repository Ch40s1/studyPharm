Calculations

Basic Pharmacy Math

  • 1 kilogram = 1,000 grams
  • 1 kilogram = 2.2 pounds
  • 1 liter = 1,000 milliliters
  • 1 gram = 1,000 milligrams
  • 1 milligram = 1,000 micrograms
  • 1 grain = 65 milligrams
  • 1 ounce = 30 grams
  • 1 teaspoon = 5 milliliters
  • 1 tablespoon = 15 milliliters
  • 1 pound = 454 grams

Roman Numerals

  • I = 1
  • V = 5
  • X = 10
  • L = 50
  • C = 100
  • D = 500
  • M = 1,000

Significant Figures Rules

  • All non-zero digits are significant.
  • Any zeros between significant digits are also significant.
  • Leading zeros are not significant.
  • Trailing zeros in a decimal number are significant.
  • Trailing zeros in a whole number with no decimal point are not significant unless specified by a bar or scientific notation.

BMI and BSA Formulas

  • BMI (Body Mass Index): BMI = weight (kg) / [height (m)]2
  • BMI (alternative formula in pounds and inches): BMI = [weight (lb) / (height (in))2] × 703
  • BSA (Body Surface Area): BSA (m2) = sqrt([height (cm) × weight (kg)] / 3600)

Temperature Conversion Formulas

  • Celsius to Fahrenheit: °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32
  • Fahrenheit to Celsius: °C = (°F - 32) × 5/9
  • Celsius to Kelvin: K = °C + 273.15
  • Kelvin to Celsius: °C = K - 273.15

Pediatric Dosage Calculations

Clark's Rule

Child's dose = (child's weight in pounds / 150) × adult dose

Young's Rule

Child's dose = (child's age in years / (child's age in years + 12)) × adult dose

Alligations

The tic-tac-toe board represents a concentration dilution guide:

  • Top left: Higher concentration solution.
  • Bottom left: Lower concentration solution.
  • Center: Desired concentration.
  • Top right: Amount of the higher concentration solution needed (calculated as higher concentration - desired concentration).
  • Bottom right: Amount of the lower concentration solution needed (calculated as lower concentration - desired concentration).

Note: If the desired concentration is higher than the initial concentrations, adjust calculations accordingly by subtracting the initial concentration from the desired value.
